# Hi, I'm Maddhav Suneja

I'm a **Computer Science major** at **NYU**, with minors in **Mathematics** and **Quantum Computing**. I’m especially interested in **data science, machine learning, AI, and backend systems**, and I like building projects that turn messy inputs into clear, usable products.

A lot of what excites me sits at the intersection of **data, systems, and interaction**: cleaning difficult datasets, designing analytical pipelines, building intelligent tools, and thinking carefully about how people actually experience the end result.

Alongside data and AI work, I’m also interested in **graphics and game development**, especially in **C++**, **OpenGL**, and shader-based rendering. I like the contrast between analytical work and low-level visual systems, and I enjoy exploring both.

## A Little About Me

I tend to be most motivated by projects that start with some kind of mess:

- inconsistent text
- unclear structure
- raw inputs that need both engineering and judgment

That’s part of why I enjoy data science and applied AI so much. I like the process of taking something chaotic, understanding it properly, and turning it into something structured, interpretable, and useful.

I’m also someone who enjoys building across domains. Some of my work leans into **data visualization and analytics**, some into **backend/product development**, and some into **graphics/game programming**. I like learning by building, and I’m happiest when a project forces me to think both technically and creatively.

## Featured Projects

### [Occupation Trends in Early New York](https://github.com/maddii28/Data-Viz-Project---Occupation-trends-in-NYC-1850-1890-)
An interactive **data visualization and geospatial analysis** project built from historical NYC directory data.

Highlights:
- cleaned and normalized noisy historical records
- geocoded addresses using the **Google Maps Geocoding API**
- engineered grouped occupation categories from inconsistent text
- built an interactive **Streamlit** dashboard with map, occupation composition, and neighborhood-level analysis

Tech:
`Python` `Pandas` `Streamlit` `Pydeck` `Plotly` `Google Maps API`

Tech:
